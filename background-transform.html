BCCI

The Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) is the governing body for cricket in India. It was established in 1928 and is responsible for promoting and developing the sport in the country.

he BCCI is headquartered in Mumbai and has its own constitution, which outlines its objectives, powers, and functions. The board is composed of various committees, including the Working Committee, the Tours and Fixtures Committee, and the Marketing Committee.

The BCCI is also responsible for selecting the Indian national cricket team and organizing domestic and international cricket tournaments in India, including the Indian Premier League (IPL). The board has played a significant role in promoting cricket in India and has contributed to the country's success in the sport.
